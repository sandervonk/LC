Q1: Helicopter Package

Question:

Q2: Train Ball Toss

Question:

A train is travelling east along a straight run of track at 59.0 km/hr59.0 \, km/hr59.0km/hr. Inside, two siblings 2.0 m2.0 \, m2.0m apart are playing catch directly across the aisle. The kid wearing a Tragically Hip T-shirt throws the ball horizontally north. The ball crosses the train and is caught 0.80 s0.80 \, s0.80s later by her little brother. (Ignore any effects of gravity or friction.)
  • Find the magnitude of the ball’s velocity from the little brother’s point of view. (in m/sm/sm/s)
  • What is the magnitude of the velocity of the ball as seen by someone standing still outside the train? (in m/sm/sm/s)
  • What is the angle between the direction of the ball and the direction of the train as measured by someone standing still outside the train? (in degdegdeg)

Variable Breakdown

d=2.0 md = 2.0 \, md=2.0m
vg,t=59.0 km/hrv_{g,t} = 59.0 \, km/hrvg,t​=59.0km/hr
vg,t=16.389 m/sv_{g,t} = 16.389 \, m/svg,t​=16.389m/s

Steps / Summary

Part 1: Magnitude of Ball’s Velocity as Little Brother

For this part of the question, since both the subjects are inside the train, we can forget about the train’s movement, and just use:

∣v∣=∣d∣t|v| = \frac{|d|}{t}∣v∣=t∣d∣​

∣vtrain,ball∣=∣dtrain,ball∣t|v_{train, ball}| = \frac{|d_{train, ball}|}{t}∣vtrain,ball​∣=t∣dtrain,ball​∣​

For my example problem, this works out to:

∣vtrain,ball∣=∣dbro,ball∣t|v_{train, ball}| = \frac{|d_{bro, ball}|}{t}∣vtrain,ball​∣=t∣dbro,ball​∣​
∣vtrain,ball∣=∣2.0 m∣0.80 s|v_{train, ball}| = \frac{|2.0\, m|}{0.80 \, s}∣vtrain,ball​∣=0.80s∣2.0m∣​
∣vtrain,ball∣=2.5 m/s|v_{train, ball}| = 2.5 \, m/s∣vtrain,ball​∣=2.5m/s

Giving me a magnitude of 2.50 m/s for this part

Part 2:

To find the velocity as observed from outside the train, we can use the same relationship as before:

v⃗a,c=v⃗a,b+v⃗b,c\vec v_{a,c} = \vec v_{a,b} + \vec v_{b,c}va,c​=va,b​+vb,c​

v⃗track,ball=v⃗track,train+v⃗train,ball\vec v_{track, ball} = \vec v_{track, train} + \vec v_{train, ball}vtrack,ball​=vtrack,train​+vtrain,ball​

∣v⃗track,ball∣=(vtrack,train,xvtrain,ball,x)2+(vtrack,train,yvtrain,ball,y)2|\vec v_{track, ball}| = \sqrt{ \left( v_{track, train, x} v_{train, ball,x} \right)^2 + \left( v_{track, train, y} v_{train, ball,y} \right)^2 }∣vtrack,ball​∣=(vtrack,train,x​vtrain,ball,x​)2+(vtrack,train,y​vtrain,ball,y​)2​

If we consider the motion of the ball to be along the x-axis, and the motion of the train as being along the y-axis, we can rewrite this as:

∣v⃗track,ball∣=∣v⃗train,ball∣2+∣v⃗track,train∣2|\vec v_{track, ball}| = \sqrt{ |\vec v_{train, ball}|^2 + |\vec v_{track, train}|^2 }∣vtrack,ball​∣=∣vtrain,ball​∣2+∣vtrack,train​∣2​

Then substitute in ∣v⃗train,ball∣|\vec v_{train, ball}|∣vtrain,ball​∣ as found in the previous part

∣v⃗track,ball∣=(∣dtrain,ball∣t)2+∣v⃗track,train∣2|\vec v_{track, ball}| = \sqrt{ \left( \frac{|d_{train, ball}|}{t} \right)^2 + |\vec v_{track, train}|^2 }∣vtrack,ball​∣=(t∣dtrain,ball​∣​)2+∣vtrack,train​∣2​

For my example problem this works out to:

∣v⃗track,ball∣=(2.50 m/s)2+∣16.389 m/s∣2|\vec v_{track, ball}| = \sqrt{ \left( 2.50 \, m/s \right)^2 + |16.389 \, m/s|^2 }∣vtrack,ball​∣=(2.50m/s)2+∣16.389m/s∣2​
∣v⃗track,ball∣=16.579 m/s|\vec v_{track, ball}| = 16.579 \, m/s∣vtrack,ball​∣=16.579m/s

Giving me an external magnitude of 16.579 m/s

Part 3: Angle of Travel

To find the angle Θ\ThetaΘ at which the ball appeared to move relative to the train, we can use the equation:

Θ=tan⁡−1(oppositeadjacent)\Theta = \tan^{-1}{\left(\frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} \right)}Θ=tan−1(adjacentopposite​)

Since the track of the train becomes the base of this triangle as per the problem text “what is the angle between the direction of the ball and the direction of the train”, the opposite side would be what we called the x-axis, perpendicular to the motion of the train, and the adjacent side would be the base of the triangle:

Θ=tan⁡−1(∣v⃗train,ball∣∣v⃗track,train∣)\Theta = \tan^{-1}{\left(\frac{|\vec v_{train, ball}|}{|\vec v_{track, train}|} \right)}Θ=tan−1(∣vtrack,train​∣∣vtrain,ball​∣​)

Θ=tan⁡−1(∣v⃗train,ball∣∣v⃗track,train∣)\Theta = \tan^{-1}{\left(\frac{|\vec v_{train, ball}|}{|\vec v_{track, train}|} \right)}Θ=tan−1(∣vtrack,train​∣∣vtrain,ball​∣​)

Which we can subsitute our earlier value into

Θ=tan⁡−1(∣dtrain,ball∣t∣v⃗track,train∣)\Theta = \tan^{-1}{\left(\frac{\frac{|d_{train, ball}|}{t}}{|\vec v_{track, train}|} \right)}Θ=tan−1(∣vtrack,train​∣t∣dtrain,ball​∣​​)

For my example problem this works out to:

Θ=tan⁡−1(∣dtrain,ball∣t∣v⃗track,train∣)\Theta = \tan^{-1}{\left(\frac{\frac{|d_{train, ball}|}{t}}{|\vec v_{track, train}|} \right)}Θ=tan−1(∣vtrack,train​∣t∣dtrain,ball​∣​​)
Θ=tan⁡−1(∣2.50 m/s∣∣16.389 m/s∣)\Theta = \tan^{-1}{\left(\frac{|2.50 \, m/s|}{|16.389 \, m/s|} \right)}Θ=tan−1(∣16.389m/s∣∣2.50m/s∣​)
Θ=8.673°\Theta = 8.673°Θ=8.673°

Giving me a final angle solution of 8.67°

A CESSNA Citation attains an airspeed of 540 km/hr540 \, km/hr540km/hr. The plane arrives in 2.00 hr2.00 \, hr2.00hr.
  • What is the wind speed? (in km/hr)
  • What is the direction of the wind? Give your answer in ° where
    • North = 0°0°0°
    • East = 90°90°90°
    • South = 180°180°180°
    • West = 270°270°270°

Variable Breakdown

Note that these are converted into the xxx-yyy coordinate plane where due East is the xxx-axis, and Θ=0°\Theta = 0°Θ=0° on the xxx-axis, and increases as we rotate counterclockwise.

∣d⃗g,p∣=1107.0 km|\vec d_{g,p}| = 1107.0 \, km∣dg,p​∣=1107.0km
t=2.00 hrt = 2.00 \, hrt=2.00hr
∣v⃗a,p∣=240 km/hr|\vec v_{a,p}| = 240 \, km/hr∣va,p​∣=240km/hr
Θa,p=70°\Theta_{a,p} = 70°Θa,p​=70°
Θg.p=90°\Theta_{g.p} = 90°Θg.p​=90°

Steps / Summary

Please note the meaning of the subscripts is as follows

  • aaa - air
  • ppp - plane
  • ggg - ground

And:

  • v⃗g,p\vec v_{g,p}vg,p​ - groundspeed / ground vector
  • v⃗g,a\vec v_{g,a}vg,a​ - windspeed / wind vector
  • v⃗a,p\vec v_{a,p}va,p​ - airspeed / air vector

Finding the Vector

To find the vector representation of the wind, we can use the formula from the previous questions:

va,c=va,b+vb,cv_{a,c} = v_{a,b} + v_{b,c}va,c​=va,b​+vb,c​

And amend it to use our subscripts

v⃗g,p=v⃗g,a+v⃗a,p\vec v_{g,p} = \vec v_{g,a} + \vec v_{a,p}vg,p​=vg,a​+va,p​

Which can then be integrated with our velocity equation for the plane’s average velocity, based on how long it took to reach the destination

v⃗=d⃗t\vec v = \frac{\vec d}{t}v=td​

v⃗g,p=d⃗g,pt\vec v_{g,p} = \frac{\vec d_{g,p}}{t}vg,p​=tdg,p​​

d⃗g,pt=v⃗g,a+v⃗a,p\frac{\vec d_{g,p}}{t} = \vec v_{g,a} + \vec v_{a,p}tdg,p​​=vg,a​+va,p​

And solve for the air vector v⃗g,a\vec v_{g,a}vg,a​:

v⃗g,a=d⃗g,pt−v⃗a,p\vec v_{g,a} = \frac{\vec d_{g,p}}{t} - \vec v_{a,p}vg,a​=tdg,p​​−va,p​

And write out the vectors in terms of their direction and magnitude components that were given:

v⃗g,a=[∣d⃗a,p∣∗cos⁡Θg,pt−∣v⃗a,p∣∗cos⁡Θa,p∣d⃗a,p∣∗sin⁡Θg,pt−∣v⃗a,p∣∗sin⁡Θa,p]\vec v_{g,a} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{|\vec d_{a,p}|*\cos{\Theta_{g,p}}}{t} - |\vec v_{a,p}|*\cos{\Theta_{a,p}} \\ \frac{|\vec d_{a,p}|*\sin{\Theta_{g,p}}}{t} - |\vec v_{a,p}|*\sin{\Theta_{a,p}} \end{bmatrix}vg,a​=[t∣da,p​∣∗cosΘg,p​​−∣va,p​∣∗cosΘa,p​t∣da,p​∣∗sinΘg,p​​−∣va,p​∣∗sinΘa,p​​]
